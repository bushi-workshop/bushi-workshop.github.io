Overview

About the Workshop

Humanoid robots are moving from laboratory settings toward deployment in homes, schools, healthcare environments, public services, and care institutions. As this transition accelerates, a central question becomes increasingly important: who are humanoids designed for?

BU-SHI aims to broaden the discussion beyond default-user assumptions and toward socially deployable humanoid systems that account for diverse users, communication styles, languages, cultures, and physical or cognitive conditions.

Rather than focusing on a single communication modality, the workshop brings together perspectives on speech-based, multimodal, embodied, and visually grounded communication systems, including sign-language-based interaction.

The goal is to develop a cross-disciplinary roadmap connecting robotics, AI, language research, HRI, and real-world deployment studies.

Scope

Topics of Interest

Social interaction design for humanoid robots
Multimodal communication: speech, gesture, gaze, embodiment
Adaptive and personalized interaction across diverse users
Cross-cultural and cross-linguistic humanoid communication
Foundation models and LLMs for embodied agents
Robust multimodal integration under real-world constraints
Interaction breakdown, repair, and turn-taking
Accessibility-aware and diversity-aware humanoid design
Evaluation beyond average performance
Ethical and societal considerations in humanoid deployment
Real-world deployment constraints
Long-term research roadmaps for socially deployable humanoids

Inclusion

Accessibility & Inclusion

This workshop is committed to fostering an inclusive, accessible, and respectful research environment aligned with the values of the IEEE RO-MAN community.

Accessibility and diversity are treated not only as logistical considerations, but as foundational design principles for socially deployable humanoids.

  • Hybrid attendance will be supported where feasible.
  • Automatic live captioning will be provided via Zoom or equivalent tools.
  • Diversity among invited speakers and panelists will be actively considered.
  • We welcome interdisciplinary and cross-community perspectives.
