Overview

Why this workshop?

As humanoid robots move toward deployment in real social environments, the key question is no longer only what they can do, but who they are designed for.

Beyond the default user

BU-SHI challenges narrow design assumptions by centering diverse users, communication styles, cultural backgrounds, and physical or cognitive conditions.

More than one modality

We bring together speech, gesture, gaze, embodied communication, multimodal dialogue, and sign-language-based interaction within a broader humanoid framework.

Roadmap-oriented discussion

The goal is not a single technical fix, but a cross-disciplinary roadmap for socially deployable humanoids across robotics, AI, HRI, language, and deployment studies.
